We wanted to share some guidelines for the poster submission, as well as a reminder that the deadline is this Friday (EOD).
Thanks!


Format: Posters should be A1 size (594 × 841 mm) in portrait/vertical orientation. Submit your poster as a print-ready PDF. We recommend designing at 150–300 DPI to ensure crisp printing.
Content: Your poster should be self-explanatory. Attendees may view it without you present. Aim to include:
a clear title and your name(s) plus contact email
a brief motivation or research question
your methodology (if relevant)
key findings or interim results
and implications or next steps.

Design: Keep it clean and readable from ~1 metre away. Use large fonts (title ≥ 60pt, body ≥ 24pt), clear headings, and plenty of white space. Visuals, diagrams, and figures are strongly encouraged (they make your work more accessible to attendees). Ideally keep a high data/ink ratio. Please keep your poster broadly consistent with ERA:AI's visual identity, which uses a minimal palette:
Primary accent: ERA's signature deep crimson/dark red — used for headings, dividers, highlights, or border elements. This is visible throughout the event document and the ERA logo.
Text: Black or very dark grey for body copy and titles.
Background: White or off-white. Avoid busy or heavily coloured backgrounds, which can reduce readability and clash with the ERA aesthetic.
Secondary tones: Dark backgrounds (charcoal or near-black) can be used sparingly for section headers or visual contrast, as seen in the event document's header imagery.

Tone The audience will be varied (including researchers, policymakers, and practitioners) from across tracks. Where possible, aim for accessible language that doesn't assume deep specialist knowledge in your specific subfield.
