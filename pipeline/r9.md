# R9: Safety-Trait Context Dependence

## Motivation

The team consensus: the current 8 behavioral traits (assertiveness, warmth, etc.) are interesting but the alignment-relevant question is whether **safety traits** — refusal, deceptiveness, power-seeking, sycophancy — are context-dependent. If a politician's refusal vector differs substantially from a teacher's, that has direct implications for universal steering approaches used in deployment.

This experiment depends on R5 (which adds safety traits + more contexts) and applies the R4 general-vs-contextual analysis specifically to safety traits, with additional safety-specific analyses.

## What It Measures

1. **Context dependence of safety traits vs. behavioral traits**: Are safety traits more or less universal than behavioral traits? Compare mean cosine-to-general for safety traits (refusal, deceptiveness, power-seeking, sycophancy) vs. behavioral traits (assertiveness, empathy, etc.).

2. **Which contexts most distort safety traits?** For each safety trait, identify the contexts whose vectors deviate most from the general direction. Are they predictable? (e.g., con_artist on deceptiveness, politician on power-seeking)

3. **Safety-trait transfer matrix**: For each safety trait, compute the transfer matrix (apply persona_A's vector to persona_B). Do safety vectors transfer worse than behavioral ones? This has direct implications for whether a "universal refusal vector" can work.

4. **Cross-trait safety correlations**: Do contexts that distort one safety trait also distort others? (e.g., does a context with unusual refusal also have unusual deceptiveness?) High correlation suggests a "safety-anomalous context" factor.

## Prerequisites

- **R5 must be complete** — safety traits must be generated, extracted, and vectorized.
- Behavioral trait results from current pipeline serve as the baseline comparison.

## Key Questions

1. Are safety traits more context-dependent than behavioral traits? If yes, "universal safety steering" is harder than assumed.
2. Which contexts most distort safety behavior? Are they the obvious ones (con_artist for deceptiveness) or surprising?
3. Do safety vectors transfer across contexts as well as behavioral ones?
4. Is there a single "safety-anomalous" factor, or do different safety traits have independent context-dependence patterns?

## Alignment Implications

If refusal vectors are highly context-dependent (e.g., cos < 0.85 between politician and teacher), then:
- Universal steering vectors for safety may need per-context calibration
- Context-dependent refusal could be a feature (different contexts warrant different refusal thresholds) or a vulnerability (adversaries can shift context to weaken refusal)
- The "general refusal direction" may be dominated by the most common training contexts, not representative of all deployment scenarios
