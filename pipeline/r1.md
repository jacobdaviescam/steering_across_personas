# R1: Bootstrap Stability

## Motivation

Contrastive vectors are computed from a finite set of activation pairs (100 per persona×trait). If a handful of outlier samples dominate the mean, the resulting vector is fragile — small changes in the dataset could flip downstream conclusions about clustering or transfer. Bootstrap resampling quantifies this risk without requiring new data.

## What It Measures

- **Pairwise stability**: cosine similarity between all pairs of bootstrap-resampled vectors for the same persona×trait. High values mean any random subset of samples yields essentially the same direction.
- **Full-data alignment**: cosine similarity between each bootstrap vector and the vector computed from all samples. High values mean the full-data vector is not an artifact of the specific sample set.

## Implementation

- **Inputs**: `outputs/{model}/activations/*.pt` (pos/neg activation pairs), `outputs/{model}/vectors/*.pt` (full-data contrastive vectors)
- **Script**: `pipeline/r1_bootstrap_vectors.py`
- **CLI**: `python pipeline/r1_bootstrap_vectors.py --activations-dir outputs/gemma-2-27b-it/activations --vectors-dir outputs/gemma-2-27b-it/vectors --layer 22`
- **Outputs**: `outputs/{model}/robustness/bootstrap/`
  - `bootstrap_stability.json` — per-pair pairwise and full-data cosine stats
  - `bootstrap_summary.json` — aggregate statistics across all persona×trait pairs
  - `bootstrap_stability_heatmap.png` — heatmap of stability scores by persona×trait
  - `bootstrap_by_trait.png` — boxplot of stability grouped by trait

**Method**: For each persona×trait pair, resamples the activation pairs with replacement 50 times and recomputes the contrastive vector each time. Measures pairwise cosine similarity across the 50 bootstrap vectors, and cosine to the original full-data vector.

## Results

Pairwise stability: **0.990 ± 0.008**. Full-data alignment: **0.995 ± 0.004**. Vectors are very stable under resampling — the contrastive directions are not driven by a few lucky samples.

## Key Questions

1. Are the steering vectors stable, or are they driven by a few lucky samples?
2. Which persona×trait combinations (if any) show low bootstrap stability, suggesting unreliable vectors?
3. Do some traits produce systematically noisier vectors than others?
