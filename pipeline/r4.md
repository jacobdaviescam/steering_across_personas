# R4: General vs Context-Dependent Vectors

## Motivation

The core question of this project: are steering vectors for a trait universal, or do they change depending on the active persona? If the "assertiveness" direction is nearly identical for a farmer and a politician, a single general vector suffices. If they diverge, context-aware steering is necessary. This experiment quantifies where each trait falls on the universal↔context-dependent spectrum.

## What It Measures

- **General vector alignment**: cosine similarity between each persona's trait vector and the "general" (context-free) vector, computed as the mean across all personas.
- **Shared/residual decomposition**: projects each persona vector onto the general direction to get the shared component, then measures the magnitude and direction of the residual (the persona-specific part).
- **Cluster bias**: runs agglomerative clustering on the transfer matrix to test whether the general vector is biased toward a particular subgroup of personas rather than being a true center.

## Implementation

- **Inputs**: `outputs/{model}/vectors/*.pt` (contrastive vectors for all persona×trait pairs)
- **Script**: `pipeline/r4_general_vs_contextual.py`
- **CLI**: `python pipeline/r4_general_vs_contextual.py --vectors-dir outputs/gemma-2-27b-it/vectors --layer 22`
- **Outputs**: `outputs/{model}/robustness/general_vs_contextual/`
  - `per_pair.json` — cosine to general vector, shared/residual magnitudes per persona×trait
  - `trait_summary.json` — aggregate context-dependence statistics per trait
  - `persona_summary.json` — aggregate deviation statistics per persona
  - `cluster_bias.json` — clustering results and bias analysis
  - `general_vs_contextual_heatmap.png` — heatmap of cosine-to-general by persona×trait
  - `trait_context_dependence.png` — ranked bar chart of context-dependence by trait

**Method**: Computes the general vector per trait by averaging all 10 persona vectors for that trait. Measures cosine between each persona's vector and this general direction. Decomposes each persona vector into its projection onto the general direction (shared component) and the orthogonal residual (context-specific component). Runs agglomerative clustering on the transfer matrix to check for sub-structure.

## Results

Most context-dependent traits: **risk_taking** (mean cosine 0.857), **impulsivity** (0.876). Most universal traits: **honesty** (0.962), **assertiveness** (0.942). Only 1 cluster found per trait with 10 personas — need more contexts (R5) for sub-structure to emerge.

Key outliers: drill_sergeant on empathy/warmth (large residual from the general direction), surgeon on risk_taking, politician on confidence. These match intuitions about which personas have the most distinctive relationship to each trait.

## Key Questions

1. Which traits are universal (single general vector suffices) vs context-dependent (persona-specific vectors needed)?
2. Is the general direction biased toward one cluster of personas, or is it a true center?
3. Which persona×trait pairs are the biggest outliers, and do they match semantic intuitions?
4. How much of each persona's vector is shared vs unique?
