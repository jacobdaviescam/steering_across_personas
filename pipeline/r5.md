# R5: Pairwise Context Similarity Matrix

## Motivation

Nathaniel's core question: can we quantify the similarity between contexts (e.g., therapist ↔ kindergarten teacher, politician ↔ lobbyist)? If semantically related contexts produce similar steering vectors, that's direct evidence for smoothness of the semantic→representation mapping. If unrelated contexts are equally similar, the vectors may just be noisy projections of the same general direction.

A pairwise similarity matrix gives finer-grained structure than clustering — even within a single cluster, we can measure relative distances and test whether they respect semantic intuitions.

## What It Measures

For each trait, compute the 10×10 (or N×N at scale) cosine similarity matrix between all persona vectors. Then:

1. **Similarity heatmap per trait** — are there visible blocks of similar personas?
2. **Mean pairwise similarity per trait** — how "universal" is each trait? (Complements r4's general-vs-contextual metric.)
3. **Semantic coherence score** — given human-labeled "similar" persona pairs (therapist↔kindergarten_teacher, politician↔lobbyist, surgeon↔drill_sergeant), do their vectors have higher cosine similarity than random pairs? Permutation test for significance.
4. **Cross-trait persona similarity** — for a given persona pair, are they similar on ALL traits or only some? E.g., therapist and kindergarten_teacher may share warmth/empathy vectors but differ on assertiveness.
